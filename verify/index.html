SaferSpeech Verification

It is important to verify that the SaferSpeech website you are visiting is authentic.
Before using SaferSpeech in a hostile environment, there are a few things you should check and be aware of.


  1. Check that you are using HTTPS
    If the "HTTPS" symbol in your browser's address bar is red, or indicates the connection is insecure, do not sign into SaferSpeech. Your connection may be compromised and an adversary could be attempting to capture your login data.
  2. Verify SaferSpeech's TLS certificate
    Depending on your browser, the method for doing this may vary.

    In Firefox, click the "lock" icon next to the address bar, click "Connection Secure", then click "More Information".
    It will open a window where you will need to click "View Certificate".
    This page will show you information about SaferSpeech's TLS certificate.

    You will need to verify that the SHA-1 fingerprint listed on the certificate matches the one here.
  3. Verify PGP Key
    The PGP key used for any communication from the developer of SaferSpeech can be found here.

    It is a good idea to save the PGP key on a non-hostile network if possible. The fingerprint of the PGP key is:

    DA24 3A2B 9139 C78C 332F D36F 36B9 A117 3DCB F7EE
